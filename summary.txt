  What you CAN do without Premiere

  - Trim / cut / split / join clips
  - Crop, resize, rotate, reframe (e.g. vertical → square)
  - Speed up / slow down, reverse
  - Concatenate multiple clips into one
  - Overlay logos, text, picture-in-picture, watermarks
  - Audio: extract, replace, mix music + voice, normalize volume, fade
  - Auto-generate + burn in captions/subtitles (Whisper → SRT → ffmpeg)
  - Color filters, brightness/contrast, blur, fades, transitions
  - Animated titles / intros / motion graphics (Remotion)
  - Compress / convert to any format for any platform

  The honest trade-offs vs Premiere

  - No visual timeline. You edit by specifying exact times/commands, not
  dragging clips. Great for precise, repeatable, or batch edits — slower for
  "feel it out" creative editing where you scrub frame-by-frame.
  - Preview loop is slower — you render to check, rather than live-scrubbing.
  - Complex multi-track creative grading/keyframing is more tedious than a GUI.

  Bottom line: For trimming, captioning, format conversion, overlays, audio
  work, and motion graphics — yes, this fully replaces Premiere for those tasks,
  and it's better for anything repeatable or automated. For free-form, highly
  visual creative cutting, a GUI is more comfortable (free options: DaVinci 
  Resolve, Shotcut, Kdenlive) — but you don't need one.

     7. Audio processing plan

     Source measured at -20.98 LUFS (input TP -1.44). Voice chain (ffmpeg, order
     matters):
     1. highpass=f=80 (remove rumble) → lowpass=f=12000 gentle.
     2. afftdn=nr=12:nf=-25 light broadband denoise/hiss (already validated
     gentle).
     3. De-hum if present: anequalizer/bandreject at 50/100Hz (verify first).
     4. De-ess: deesser (or targeted firequalizer 5–8kHz).
     5. Presence EQ: gentle +2–3dB around 3–5kHz, slight 150Hz cut for mud.
     6. Compression: acompressor=threshold=-18dB:ratio=3:attack=10:release=120
     for consistency.
     7. Loudness: two-pass loudnorm I=-14:TP=-1.5:LRA=11 (reuse measured stats).
     8. alimiter=limit=-1.0dB safety.

     Music bed: royalty-free corporate/legal track, looped/trimmed to length,
     set to ~-24 LUFS, side-chain ducked to voice (sidechaincompress) so it dips
     under speech. SFX: impact/riser (hook), whoosh (transitions), pop (term

     8. Color grading plan (tonemap HLG→SDR + premium grade)

     ffmpeg (zscale/zimg in the Gyan build):
     1. Tonemap: zscale=t=linear:npl=100, format=gbrpf32le, zscale=p=bt709, 
     tonemap=hable:desat=0, zscale=t=bt709:m=bt709:r=tv, format=yuv420p → clean
     Rec.709 SDR.
     2. White balance: neutralize warm office cast
     (colorbalance/colortemperature).
     3. Contrast: gentle S-curve (curves=preset=medium_contrast or custom),
     protect highlights (bookshelf) and lift shadows slightly.

     (avoid heavy blur — trust content).
